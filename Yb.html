Ytterbium (Yb)

Basic Information

Atomic Number: 70

Atomic Mass: 173.04

Symbol: Yb

Atomic Structure

  • Protons: 70
  • Neutrons: ~103 (varies by isotope)
  • Electrons: 70

Electron Configuration

[Xe] 4f14 6s2

Isotopes

  • Yb-168, Yb-170, Yb-171, Yb-172, Yb-173, Yb-174, Yb-176 (most are stable)

Physical Properties

  • State: Solid
  • Melting Point: 824 °C
  • Boiling Point: 1196 °C
  • Density: 6.90 g/cm³

Chemical Properties

  • Soft, silvery metal
  • Reacts slowly with water and air
  • Typically forms Yb2+ and Yb3+ ions

Occurrence

Found in minerals like monazite and xenotime; rare earth element.

Applications

  • Used in lasers, atomic clocks, and stainless steel production
  • Acts as a radiation source for portable X-ray machines

Historical Background

Discovered by Jean Charles Galissard de Marignac in 1878 in Switzerland; named after the Swedish village Ytterby.

Safety & Toxicity

Ytterbium metal is relatively safe, but fine powder form may pose fire or explosion hazards. Compounds should be handled with care.

Position in Periodic Table

Group: Lanthanides, Period: 6, f-block (rare earth element)

Interesting Facts

Ytterbium atomic clocks are so accurate they could deviate by only a second over the age of the universe.
