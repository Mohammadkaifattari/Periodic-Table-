Sulfur (S)

Basic Information

Atomic Number: 16

Atomic Mass: 32.06

Symbol: S

Atomic Structure

  • Protons: 16
  • Neutrons: Usually 16 (varies by isotope)
  • Electrons: 16

Electron Configuration

[Ne] 3s2 3p4

Isotopes

  • S-32 (most abundant)
  • S-33
  • S-34
  • S-36

Physical Properties

  • State: Solid
  • Melting Point: 115.21 °C
  • Boiling Point: 444.6 °C
  • Density: 2.07 g/cm³

Chemical Properties

  • Non-metal
  • Reacts with oxygen to form sulfur dioxide (SO₂)
  • Forms sulfides with metals

Occurrence

Sulfur occurs naturally in volcanic regions, hot springs, and is commonly found in minerals and ores like pyrite (FeS₂). It is also present in fossil fuels.

Applications

  • Used in the production of sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄)
  • Used in vulcanization of rubber
  • Used in fertilizers, detergents, and pharmaceuticals

Historical Background

Sulfur has been known since ancient times, often referred to as brimstone. It was used in ancient medicines and religious rituals. The name comes from the Latin "sulphur."

Safety & Toxicity

Elemental sulfur is non-toxic, but sulfur dioxide gas (SO₂) and hydrogen sulfide (H₂S) are toxic and can be dangerous when inhaled.
