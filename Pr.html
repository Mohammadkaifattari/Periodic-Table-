Praseodymium (Pr)

Basic Information

Atomic Number: 59

Atomic Mass: 140.91

Symbol: Pr

Atomic Structure

  • Protons: 59
  • Neutrons: ~82 (varies by isotope)
  • Electrons: 59

Electron Configuration

[Xe] 4f3 6s2

Isotopes

  • Pr-141: Only naturally occurring and stable isotope

Physical Properties

  • State: Solid
  • Melting Point: 931 °C
  • Boiling Point: 3520 °C
  • Density: 6.77 g/cm³

Chemical Properties

  • Reacts slowly with oxygen and quickly with acids
  • Forms a green oxide layer when exposed to air

Occurrence

Occurs naturally in monazite and bastnäsite minerals. Found along with other rare earth elements.

Applications

  • Used in high-strength metals for aircraft engines
  • Coloring agent in glasses and ceramics (green tint)
  • Component of Mischmetal for flints in lighters
  • Magnets and carbon arc lighting

Historical Background

Discovered in 1885 by Carl Auer von Welsbach. The name comes from the Greek word "prasios" meaning green and "didymos" meaning twin.

Safety & Toxicity

Low toxicity but should be handled with care in powder form. Dust can be flammable and pose inhalation risk.

Position in Periodic Table

Group: Lanthanides, Period: 6, Block: f

Interesting Facts

Praseodymium gives glass and enamel a bright green color. It is also used to create strong permanent magnets when alloyed with neodymium.
