Neptunium (Np)

Basic Information

Atomic Number: 93

Atomic Mass: 237.048

Symbol: Np

Atomic Structure

  • Protons: 93
  • Neutrons: Varies by isotope (Np-237 has 144)
  • Electrons: 93

Electron Configuration

[Rn] 5f4 6d1 7s2

Isotopes

  • Np-237 (most common and long-lived)
  • Several synthetic isotopes with short half-lives

Physical Properties

  • State: Solid
  • Melting Point: 639 °C
  • Boiling Point: 4000 °C (approximate)
  • Density: 20.45 g/cm³

Chemical Properties

  • Radioactive and reactive with oxygen, acids, and halogens
  • Can form multiple oxidation states (+3 to +7)

Occurrence

Neptunium is a synthetic element, first produced in nuclear reactors. Trace amounts can be found in uranium ores due to neutron capture.

Applications

  • Used in scientific research
  • Precursor for producing plutonium-238 (used in space probes)

Historical Background

Discovered in 1940 by Edwin McMillan and Philip Abelson at the Berkeley Radiation Laboratory. Named after the planet Neptune.

Safety & Toxicity

Highly radioactive and chemically toxic. Requires specialized handling and shielding. Poses environmental and health hazards if not contained properly.
